🥩 Steak and Veg

High-protein steak plate with roasted sweet potato, greens, and fresh chimichurri.

Servings

1 serving

Quick Stats

⏱ 30–40 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 1 serving

Ingredients

  • 250g steak (sirloin or ribeye)
  • 200g sweet potato
  • 100g broccolini
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 15g parsley
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp vinegar or lemon juice

Method

  1. Roast sweet potato:
    Cut into chunks, season, and roast at high heat until crispy outside and soft inside. This provides your slow-digesting carbohydrate base.
  2. Prepare chimichurri:
    Mix parsley, garlic, olive oil, vinegar, and salt. Let it sit so flavours meld while you cook the rest.
  3. Cook the steak properly:
    Season generously and sear in a very hot pan. Cook to preferred doneness, then rest for 5–10 minutes — this is essential for juice retention.
  4. Cook broccolini:
    Lightly steam or blanch first, then finish in a hot pan with garlic for flavour and texture.
  5. Assemble plate:
    Slice steak against the grain. Serve with roasted sweet potato, broccolini, and spoon over chimichurri.

Key Nutrients & Benefits

High-quality protein from steak supports muscle growth and recovery, sweet potato provides slow-release carbohydrates and potassium, while chimichurri delivers antioxidants and healthy fats from olive oil.

Macros (approx per serve)

  • Calories: 550–800 kcal
  • Protein: 40–55g (very high)
  • Carbs: 30–50g (sweet potato)
  • Fats: 25–45g (steak + oil)

Smart Swaps & Variations

  • Chicken thighs instead of steak — cheaper, slightly lower fat option.
  • Swap sweet potato for white potato — lighter flavour, crispier roast.
  • Add avocado — increases healthy fats and creaminess.
  • Add chili flakes to chimichurri — extra heat and metabolism boost.
  • Swap broccolini for asparagus or green beans — seasonal flexibility.

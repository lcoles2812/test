🍜 Pad Kee Mao (Drunken Noodles)

A bold, fast weeknight noodle dish with chilli, garlic, soy, and fresh basil, built to be punchy and satisfying without being too fussy.

This is one of the meals I like to make when I want something quick and proper, and it’s easy to change up depending on what’s around by swapping in different vegetables like capsicum, green beans, or whatever else needs using.

It’s a great one for a busy night when you still want something big on flavour and a little bit of texture.

Servings

2 servings

Quick Stats

⏱ 25–30 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 300g chicken thigh strips
  • 200g rice noodles
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp ginger
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 10g Thai basil
  • 2 spring onions

Method

  1. Prep everything first (wok cooking is fast):
    Slice chicken and prepare all sauces before turning on heat. Once cooking starts, everything moves quickly.
  2. Prepare noodles:
    Soak or boil rice noodles until just tender. Drain and toss lightly with oil to prevent sticking.
  3. Sear the chicken:
    Heat a wok or large pan until very hot. Cook chicken in a single layer until browned and just cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  4. Build aromatics base:
    In the same pan, fry garlic, ginger, and chilli in oil. Cook briefly (30–60 seconds) until fragrant — do not burn.
  5. Build the sauce:
    Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and honey. Let it bubble for 1–2 minutes to slightly reduce and thicken.
  6. Combine everything:
    Add noodles and chicken back into the wok. Toss continuously until noodles are fully coated and glossy.
  7. Finish fresh:
    Turn off heat and add Thai basil and spring onion. Toss lightly — residual heat is enough to release aroma without overcooking herbs.

Key Nutrients & Benefits

High protein from chicken supports muscle repair, garlic and chilli support immune and metabolic function, while basil provides antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Macros (approx per serve)

  • Calories: 450–650 kcal
  • Protein: 30–40g (high)
  • Carbs: 50–80g (mainly noodles)
  • Fats: 10–20g (light, sauce-based)

Smart Swaps & Variations

  • Beef or tofu instead of chicken — changes protein profile while keeping sauce structure.
  • Extra veg (broccoli, bok choy) — increases fibre and volume.
  • Low-sugar version — reduce or remove honey.
  • Gluten-free version — use tamari instead of soy sauce.
  • Extra spice — add fresh chilli or chilli oil at the end.

🌮 Chipotle Chicken Tacos

Smoky chipotle-marinated chicken with lime, garlic and paprika, packed into warm tortillas and topped with guac, feta, diced onion and tomato.

This one is quick enough for a weeknight but still tastes like a proper taco night. Marinate while you prep toppings, then cook the chicken hot and fast for char and flavour.

Servings

4 servings

Quick Stats

⏱ 25–35 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 600g chicken thighs, sliced
  • 2 tbsp chipotle in adobo
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lime (juice + zest)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 0.5 tsp black pepper
  • 12 small tortillas
  • 1 cup guacamole
  • 120g feta, crumbled
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced

Method

  1. Marinate the chicken:
    Mix chipotle in adobo, garlic, paprika, olive oil, lime juice and zest, salt, and pepper. Coat the chicken well and marinate for at least 15 minutes.
  2. Prep toppings:
    Dice onion and tomato, crumble feta, and portion guacamole so everything is ready to build quickly.
  3. Cook chicken hot and fast:
    Heat a large pan over medium-high heat and cook chicken in batches until caramelised and cooked through. Rest for 2-3 minutes, then chop into taco-size pieces.
  4. Warm tortillas:
    Heat tortillas in a dry pan or directly over flame for a few seconds each side until soft and slightly charred.
  5. Assemble tacos:
    Fill tortillas with chicken, then top with guac, feta, diced onion and tomato. Finish with extra lime if you want more brightness.

Key Nutrients & Benefits

Chicken provides high-quality protein, tomato and lime add vitamin C, and avocado-based guac contributes healthy fats for satiety.

Macros (approx per serve)

  • Calories: 550–700 kcal
  • Protein: 35–45g
  • Carbs: 35–55g
  • Fats: 25–35g

Smart Swaps & Variations

  • Use chicken breast for a leaner version.
  • Swap feta for cotija if you want a more classic taco-style finish.
  • Add shredded lettuce for extra crunch and freshness.
  • Use Greek yogurt + lime if you want a lighter topping than guac.

🥩 Korean Beef Mince Rice Bowl

A glossy Korean-inspired rice bowl with garlicky beef mince, spring onion, chilli, sesame and a creamy sriracha yoghurt sauce.

It’s the kind of weeknight bowl that tastes bigger than the effort it takes: savoury mince, fresh rice, and two quick sauces that pull everything together.

Servings

4 servings

Quick Stats

⏱ 30–35 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 4 servings

Ingredients

Beef Mince

  • 700g beef mince
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar or honey
  • 1 tbsp gochujang or chilli paste
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Rice Bowl

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine or short grain rice
  • 3 stalks spring onion, sliced
  • 1 red chilli, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds

Sriracha Mayo

  • 0.5 cup mayonnaise
  • 1.5 tbsp sriracha
  • 1 tsp lime juice

Greek Yoghurt Sauce

  • 250g Greek yoghurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely grated
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp chopped spring onion or parsley

Optional Additions

  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 0.5 cup kimchi
  • 1 tsp extra chilli oil

Method

  1. Cook the rice:
    Prepare rice according to packet instructions and keep it warm.
  2. Mix the sauces:
    Whisk together the sriracha mayo and the Greek yoghurt sauce in separate bowls. Thin with a splash of water if needed.
  3. Brown the beef:
    Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add beef mince and cook until browned, breaking it up as it cooks.
  4. Add flavour:
    Stir in garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, gochujang and black pepper. Cook until the sauce is glossy and the mince is sticky.
  5. Finish with aromatics:
    Fold through spring onion and most of the sliced chilli just before serving.
  6. Assemble:
    Divide rice between bowls, top with beef mince, then drizzle over sriracha mayo and Greek yoghurt sauce. Finish with sesame seeds, spring onion and extra chilli.

Plating Order

Rice → beef mince → sriracha mayo → Greek yoghurt sauce → spring onion → chilli → sesame.

Approx Nutrition (per serve)

  • Protein: 40-45g
  • Calories: 650-800 kcal
  • High in: iron, zinc, B12, calcium

Swaps & Additions

  • Swap the rice: Use brown rice, cauliflower rice, or a half-and-half mix.
  • More veg: Add shredded cabbage, edamame, or cucumber ribbons.
  • Heat level: Add extra sriracha, gochujang, or a drizzle of chilli oil.
  • Finish: Add pickled onion or kimchi if you want extra tang and crunch.

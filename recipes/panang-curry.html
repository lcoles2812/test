🍛 Panang Curry

Creamy Thai curry with rich coconut, subtle sweetness, and deep spice.

Servings

3 servings

Quick Stats

⏱ 30–40 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 3 servings

Ingredients

  • 600g chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp Panang curry paste
  • 400ml coconut cream/milk
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 capsicum
  • 100g green beans
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)

Method

  1. Sear the chicken:
    Heat a pan over medium-high heat. Add chicken and brown lightly — this builds flavour before the sauce is added. Remove and set aside.
  2. Fry the curry paste (key step):
    Add a little oil, then fry Panang curry paste for 1–2 minutes. This step “blooms” the spices and releases deep aromatic oils.
  3. Build the base:
    Slowly add coconut cream while stirring. Mix until the paste fully dissolves and forms a smooth, rich sauce.
  4. Simmer vegetables:
    Add garlic, capsicum, and green beans. Let them simmer gently so they soften slightly but still retain texture.
  5. Return chicken:
    Add chicken back into the sauce. Simmer until fully cooked and the sauce slightly thickens.
  6. Balance flavour:
    Add fish sauce for saltiness and depth. Add a small pinch of sugar only if needed to balance heat and richness.
  7. Final texture check:
    The sauce should be thick, glossy, and cling to the chicken. Adjust consistency with a splash of water if too thick.

Key Nutrients & Benefits

High protein from chicken supports muscle repair, coconut provides healthy fats for energy, and capsicum + green beans add fibre, antioxidants, and vitamin C for immune support.

Macros (approx per serve)

  • Calories: 550–750 kcal
  • Protein: 30–40g
  • Carbs: 10–25g
  • Fats: 35–55g (coconut-based)

Smart Swaps & Variations

  • Beef instead of chicken — richer, deeper flavour profile.
  • Tofu version — absorbs curry sauce well for vegetarian option.
  • Extra vegetables — zucchini, eggplant, or spinach increase volume and fibre.
  • Lighter version — use light coconut milk instead of cream.
  • Extra heat — add fresh chilli or chilli oil at the end.

🌯 Burrito Bowl

Smoky chipotle chicken with fresh toppings, built for high protein and meal prep.

Servings

4 servings

Quick Stats

⏱ 30–40 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 400g chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp chipotle in adobo
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 300g rice
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 cup corn
  • 1 capsicum
  • 1 onion
  • 150g Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp mayo

Method

Method

  1. Marinate the chicken (build flavour early):
    In a bowl, mix chipotle in adobo, garlic, cumin, paprika, lime juice, salt, and a splash of oil. Coat the chicken thoroughly and let it sit for at least 20–30 minutes (longer if you have time). This step is where the smoky, spicy base flavour develops.
  2. Cook the rice properly:
    Rinse rice until the water runs mostly clear to remove excess starch. Cook as normal, then finish with a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lime juice for brightness. Fluff and set aside.
  3. Sear the chicken (don’t overcrowd the pan):
    Heat a pan until hot, then add chicken in a single layer. Let it sit undisturbed for a few minutes to develop a charred crust before flipping. Cook until fully done, then rest for 5 minutes before slicing — this keeps it juicy.
  4. Cook the vegetables for texture:
    In the same pan, sauté onion and capsicum over medium-high heat. You want light charring on the edges while still keeping some crunch. This adds contrast to the soft rice and chicken.
  5. Build the sauce (balance is key):
    Mix Greek yogurt with a small amount of mayo, a spoon of chipotle marinade, and lime juice. Taste and adjust — it should be creamy, tangy, and slightly smoky, not heavy.
  6. Prep the toppings:
    Drain black beans and warm them slightly. Do the same with corn if using frozen or canned. This ensures the bowl isn’t cold in some parts and hot in others.
  7. Assemble the bowl (layer for texture):
    Start with rice as the base. Add sliced chicken, then beans, corn, and sautéed vegetables. Finish with sauce on top so it coats everything as you mix.
  8. Final balance check:
    Taste and adjust with extra lime juice, salt, or a bit more sauce. The goal is a balance of smoky, fresh, creamy, and spicy in every bite.

Key Nutrients & Benefits

High in protein from chicken for muscle repair, fibre from beans for digestion, and vitamin C from capsicum and lime supporting immune health and recovery.

Macros (approx per serve)

  • Calories: 500–650 kcal
  • Protein: 35–45g (high, supports recovery)
  • Carbs: 50–80g (rice + beans provide energy)
  • Fats: 15–25g (yogurt + chicken + mayo)

Smart Swaps & Variations

  • Pork mince instead of chicken — richer, slightly higher fat, deeper flavour.
  • Add spinach — boosts iron, fibre, and micronutrients with no flavour change.
  • Brown rice instead of white — more fibre, slower energy release.
  • Extra beans — increases protein + fibre, makes it more filling.
  • Avocado topping — adds healthy fats and creaminess.

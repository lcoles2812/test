🍤 Garlic Chilli Prawn Pasta

A bright, silky pasta built on garlic, chilli, lemon, and sweet prawns, with a sauce that feels rich without being heavy.

Sometimes I like to throw in a handful of cherry tomatoes for a little extra sweetness and colour, and it’s one of those dishes that feels especially good on a Saturday night when I’ve got the time to make pasta from scratch in the afternoon.

It’s also a great one to cook for other people because it comes together quickly, looks impressive, and always feels a bit special.

Servings

2 servings

Quick Stats

⏱ 20–25 mins • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 Base: 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 200g prawns
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 100ml white wine
  • 1 lemon (juice)
  • 200g pasta
  • 10g parsley

Method

  1. Cook pasta properly:
    Boil pasta in heavily salted water until al dente. Reserve at least one cup of pasta water before draining — this is key for the sauce.
  2. Sear prawns quickly:
    Heat pan until very hot. Cook prawns for 1–2 minutes per side until just pink. Remove immediately to avoid overcooking — they will finish in the sauce.
  3. Build flavour base:
    Lower heat slightly and fry garlic and chilli in olive oil. Cook gently to avoid burning — this forms the flavour foundation.
  4. Deglaze with wine:
    Add white wine and scrape the pan. Let it reduce by about half to concentrate flavour and remove sharp alcohol notes.
  5. Create the sauce (important step):
    Add lemon juice and a splash of reserved pasta water. Stir vigorously — the starch in the water emulsifies with oil to create a light, glossy sauce.
  6. Combine everything:
    Add pasta and prawns back into the pan. Toss continuously until everything is coated evenly in sauce.
  7. Finish fresh:
    Turn off heat and add parsley. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or extra lemon juice if needed.

Key Nutrients & Benefits

High-quality lean protein from prawns supports muscle recovery, garlic provides immune support, lemon adds vitamin C, and olive oil provides healthy fats for heart health.

Macros (approx per serve)

  • Calories: 400–550 kcal
  • Protein: 25–35g
  • Carbs: 45–70g
  • Fats: 8–18g (light, olive oil based)

Smart Swaps & Variations

  • Wholewheat pasta — higher fibre, slower digestion.
  • Chilli oil finish — adds deeper heat and richness.
  • Add spinach or rocket — increases nutrients and freshness.
  • Swap prawns for chicken — more filling, slightly higher fat.
  • Gluten-free pasta — keeps dish accessible without changing flavour profile.
